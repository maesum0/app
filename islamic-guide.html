نور الإسلام
Noor — Light of Islam
A gentle introduction to the world's second-largest faith
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ
In the name of God, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful

Islam is a monotheistic faith revealed to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in 7th-century Arabia. The word Islam means "submission to God" (Allah), and its followers — Muslims — seek peace through worship, good character, and service to humanity.

🌍 الأمة الإسلامية
1.8 Billion Muslims

Islam is practiced across every continent, making it the world's second-largest religion, with diverse cultures united by shared faith.

📅 بداية الإسلام
Founded 610 CE

The first revelation came to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the cave of Hira near Mecca, beginning 23 years of divine guidance.

☮️ السلام
A Religion of Peace

The Islamic greeting "As-salamu alaykum" — peace be upon you — reflects the central value of harmony, compassion, and mercy.

📖 الوحي
Abrahamic Faith

Islam acknowledges and honors all previous prophets including Abraham, Moses, and Jesus, seeing itself as the completion of their message.

أركان الإسلام الخمسة

The Five Pillars of Islam

The core acts of worship that structure a Muslim's life

١
الشَّهَادَة
Shahada — Declaration of Faith

The testimony that "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His messenger." This sincere declaration marks entry into Islam and is the foundation of Muslim identity.

٢
الصَّلَاة
Salah — Daily Prayer

Muslims pray five times a day — at dawn, noon, afternoon, sunset, and night — facing the Kaaba in Mecca. Prayer connects the believer directly to God throughout the day.

٣
الزَّكَاة
Zakat — Almsgiving

An annual charity of 2.5% of one's savings given to those in need. Zakat purifies wealth, reduces inequality, and expresses solidarity with the less fortunate.

٤
الصَّوْم
Sawm — Fasting

During the holy month of Ramadan, Muslims abstain from food, drink, and wrongdoing from dawn to sunset. Fasting cultivates self-discipline, gratitude, and empathy for the hungry.

٥
الحَجّ
Hajj — Pilgrimage

At least once in a lifetime, every able Muslim journeys to Mecca to perform ancient rites. Hajj symbolizes the unity of Muslims worldwide and their equality before God.

أركان الإيمان الستة

The Six Articles of Faith

Core beliefs that define Islamic theology

Belief in Allah (God)

Tawhid — the absolute oneness of God. Allah has no partners, no children, and no equals. He is the Creator, Sustainer, and Lord of all existence.

Belief in the Angels (Malaika)

Angels are created from light, serve God without question, and carry out His commands — including delivering revelation, recording deeds, and guarding over souls.

Belief in the Scriptures (Kutub)

God sent divine books to humanity, including the Torah (Tawrat), the Psalms (Zabur), the Gospel (Injeel), and the final scripture — the Quran.

Belief in the Prophets (Anbiya)

God sent 124,000 prophets to guide humanity — including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and finally Muhammad ﷺ, the Seal of the Prophets.

Belief in the Day of Judgment (Yawm al-Qiyamah)

All of creation will be resurrected and held accountable for their deeds. Those who believed and did good will enter paradise; those who rejected truth will face consequence.

Belief in Divine Decree (Qadar)

God has knowledge of all things past, present, and future. Muslims believe in trusting God's plan while exercising their own free will and taking responsibility for choices.

📖
114 Chapters (Surahs)

The Quran contains 114 chapters and 6,236 verses covering theology, law, history, ethics, and guidance for all of life's situations.

🎵
Recited with Tajweed

Muslims recite the Quran with precise rules of pronunciation called Tajweed, preserving its sound exactly as revealed 1,400 years ago.

🕌
Memorized by Millions

Those who memorize the entire Quran are called Huffaz. Millions of Muslims worldwide have committed the entire text to memory.

Quran 20:14
🧼
Wudu — Ritual Purification

Before prayer, Muslims wash their hands, mouth, face, arms, head, and feet in a specific order called wudu, symbolizing spiritual and physical cleanliness.

🧭
Facing the Qibla

All prayers are performed facing the Kaaba in Mecca — uniting over a billion Muslims in a single direction of worship.

🕌
Friday Jumu'ah

Friday's midday prayer holds special importance. Muslims gather in mosques for a communal prayer and sermon (khutbah) reflecting on faith and life.

القيم الإسلامية

Islamic Values & Ethics

The moral compass Islam teaches its followers

💚 الرحمة
Rahma — Compassion

God is described as Al-Rahman, Al-Raheem — the Most Compassionate, Most Merciful. Muslims are called to embody mercy in all relationships.

⚖️ العدل
Adl — Justice

Islam commands standing for justice even against oneself or one's family. Equality before God is a cornerstone of Islamic social ethics.

🤝 الأخوة
Ukhuwwa — Brotherhood

All Muslims are brothers and sisters in faith. The Prophet ﷺ said: "None of you truly believes until he wishes for his brother what he wishes for himself."

🌿 الصدق
Sidq — Truthfulness

Honesty in word and deed is a defining quality of a believer. The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was known as Al-Amin — The Trustworthy — before prophethood.

🌱 الأمانة
Amanah — Trustworthiness

Fulfilling one's responsibilities — to God, family, community, and the earth — is a sacred duty. Even the natural world is held in trust (khalifa) by humanity.

🙏 الشكر
Shukr — Gratitude

Gratitude to God for all blessings is central to Islamic spirituality. The Quran says: "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you in favor."

إِنَّمَا بُعِثْتُ لِأُتَمِّمَ مَكَارِمَ الْأَخْلَاقِ

"I was sent only to perfect noble character." — Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
